                                                                              Lautenberg Opposes Decision To Deposit Dredge Spoils In Palmyra Cove                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                              Senator Says Palmyra Cove Nature Park Should Be Protected                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                          Contact  Press Office  202  224 3224 Monday  August 13  2007      WASHINGTON  D C   Sen  Frank R  Lautenberg  D NJ  today issued the following statement after learning that the New Jersey Department of Environmental Protection  DEP  plans to deposit 20 acres of dredge spoils in Palmyra Cove Nature Park   The DEPs plan to deposit dredge spoils in the Palmyra Cove Nature Park is unacceptable  I oppose this plan wholeheartedly  The Park  its native animals and plants species deserve better than to be treated like a waste dump   The U S  Fish and Wildlife Service  environmental groups  and local residents familiar with the region offered substantial alternatives today that should be considered by the U S  Army Corps of Engineers and DEP  In light of todays public information session and the frustration shown by residents and park supporters  I urge DEP and the Corps to hold formal public hearings to consider those alternatives                         Questions or Comments

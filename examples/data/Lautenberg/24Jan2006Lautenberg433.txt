                                                                              Simply Adds More Red Tape to Disastrous Start Up of New Medicare Drug Plan  says Lawmaker                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                          Tuesday  January 24  2006      WASHINGTON  DC    This afternoon  United States Senator Frank R  Lautenberg  D NJ  reacted to reports that HHS Secretary Michael Leavitt has agreed to reimburse states for the costs incurred as a result of the Federal government s failures in the start up of the new Medicare prescription drug plan         This is no solution    it is simply more red tape from the Bush Administration  If anything  today s announcement only makes the problem worse  First  states had to step in and spend millions to prevent the Medicare system from collapsing at the beginning of this year  Now the Federal Government wants the states to act as its bill collectors  The burden on the states keeps growing  when the Federal Government should just do its job and pay back the states immediately       Congress must quickly enact legislation to require the Federal Government to pay the states back directly  My bill already has 24 senators on board and support is growing by the day                          Questions or Comments
